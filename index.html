Overview of Vietnamese cuisine

Vietnam is an agricultural country belonging to the hot, tropical monsoon region. Moreover, the territory is divided into three distinct regions: North, Central and South, along with 54 ethnic groups. The characteristics of geography, culture, ethnicity and climate have determined the specific characteristics of the cuisine of each region. Each region has its own distinctive character and distinctive taste. The article Vietnemese Food: What to Eat in VietNam will help audiences have a new look of Vietnamese cuisine.

Abundant source of ingredients

As a sub-equatorial country, Vietnam has a rich source of ingredients from animals to plants. Each region in Vietnam has special raw ingredients that are different from the rest. For example, the main source of raw materials in the South is freshwater fish and fruit trees, while in the Central region, there is an abundant source of seafood. The North is a combination of the other two regions with sources of freshwater fish, seafood and plants. With a rich source of ingredients from nature, Vietnamese people have created many dishes with very special flavors.

Vietnamese daily meals

A Vietnamese family meal basically always includes the following dishes: meat, vegetarian, soup and rice. Each dish has a certain amount of nutrients, while others have their own nutritional content.In a meal, Vietnamese people use rice as the main ingredient, combined with other dishes. The combination of dishes is also a way to prevent the meal from repeating, thereby helping the eaters not to lose their appetite. They can combine rice with meat dishes, or rice with vegetables, or soups, they can even combine them all still ensure the deliciousness of the meal.
